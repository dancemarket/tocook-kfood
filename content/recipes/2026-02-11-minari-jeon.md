## The Sound of Rain: Minari-Jeon

In Korea, whenever it rains, people instinctively crave two things: **Jeon** (pancakes) and **Makgeolli** (rice wine). The sizzling sound of frying a pancake mimics the sound of falling raindrops, creating a cozy and nostalgic atmosphere. Among many varieties, **Minari-Jeon** stands out for its incredible aroma and refreshing "green" taste.

### The Magic of Minari
Minari, or Water Parsley, became globally famous through the award-winning film *Minari*. It symbolizes resilience as it grows well anywhere with water. In Korean cuisine, it is prized for its ability to detoxify the body and its unique celery-like fragrance that cuts through the heaviness of fried foods.

### How to get the perfect Crunch
The biggest mistake people make is using too much flour. A "High Value" Minari-Jeon is 90% vegetable and 10% batter. You want just enough flour to glue the stems together. When you bite into it, you should feel the fresh pop of the Minari juice followed by the salty crunch of the thin coating.

### Dipping Sauce Recommendation
Mix **2 tbsp Soy Sauce, 1 tbsp Vinegar, 1 tsp Chilli flakes, and half an onion (diced)**. The acidity of the vinegar perfectly balances the oiliness of the pancake.

Whether it's a rainy day or just a craving for something fresh, this Minari-Jeon will bring a piece of Korean spring directly to your home.
