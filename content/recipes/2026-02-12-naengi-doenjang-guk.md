## The Scent of Spring: Naengi-Doenjangguk

In the Korean countryside, as soon as the frozen ground begins to soften, you will see people out in the fields with small baskets and knives. They are looking for **Naengi** (Shepherd's Purse). This wild green is prized not for its leaves, but for its sturdy root which carries the concentrated essence of the earth's awakening.

### Why this dish is special
This soup is more than just food; it is a ritual of health. Naengi is incredibly rich in protein, calcium, and iron, making it the perfect remedy for 'Chun-gon-jeung' (Spring Fatigue). When combined with the probiotics of fermented **Doenjang**, it creates a powerful tonic for the body.

### 🌾 The Hero Ingredient: Naengi (Shepherd's Purse)
Shepherd's purse is one of the most representative 'Spring Greens' (Bom-namul) in Korea. It has a slightly bitter yet sweet aftertaste and a distinct woody aroma that is irreplaceable.

### Culinary Notes
*   **The Broth:** If you don't have anchovy broth, use the water leftover from washing rice (Ssal-tteu-mul). It neutralizes the strong smell of the soybean paste and adds a subtle creaminess.
*   **The Scent:** To keep the Naengi fragrant, add it toward the end of the cooking process. Over-boiling will kill the fresh scent.

Enjoy this bowl of Korean tradition and feel the warmth of spring in every spoonful.
